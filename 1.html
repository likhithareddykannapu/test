Machine-Driven Global Education Review:Examining Economic Growth Influences on Access And Equity

, "Machine-Driven Global Education Review," explores how economic growth affects how people can get an education and whether everyone has equal opportunities. It examines if economic progress makes it easier for more people to go to school, receive quality education, and have a fair chance to succeed. The review also looks at how economic changes can impact the ways we teach and learn in schools and how these changes affect how we develop our skills for the job market. Ultimately, the goal is to understand how we can use economic progress to create a more inclusive and equitable education system for everyone globally.
